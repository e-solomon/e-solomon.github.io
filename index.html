My Education

I am a dual major Mechanical Engineer and Programs in Design and Innovation (PDI) student at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. PDI is an interdisciplinary program which teaches the design process through a series of studio courses. We learn not only about the design process and how to apply it to real world problems, but also to consider the social and societal ramifications of our design. For example, typical stakeholder analysis for a business focuses on designing for people in positions of power. However, people in powerful positions have societal and political standing to make thier opinions known. People without that powerful voice are often ignored by society, despite the fact that they may be directly impacted by a design or program that is implemented. Combining the design and ethics education of the PDI program with the technical knowledge of mechanical engineering makes me a well-rounded student and a well-rounded person. I have the ability to consider an issue from many different perspectives, in order to determine the best course of action to remedy the problem.

My Projects

Design for America is a national club with chapters on 36 campuses. We use human centered design to address problems affecting our communities. This nationally sponsored project focused on family food security. We designed a shelf-stable meal kit that could be purchased in stores or picked up through a food pantry. It contains all of the main ingredients and cooking instructions necessary to make a meal for 4-5 people. This streamlines the process of making dinner for families on the go without breaking the bank. It also provides structure for people who get meals from a food pantry because it ensures that families can make a square meal out of the food they are provided.

The Programs in Design and Innovation classes are a series of studio courses which encourage students to think outside the box and explore their interests as they relate to design. I believe biomimicry is one of the most promising ways for humans to improve thier lives and solve societal problems. There are many marine species that bioluminesce: or glow in the dark. Bioluminescent bacteria can be used to determine the level of pollution in a water source. The brightness of the luminescense of the bacterium can be measured with a photometer and related to a pollution level. Hoosick Falls, New York is one town that was told for 18 months that their water was okay to drink. However, the EPA then told them that there were carcinogens in the water during the 19th month. I proposed a citizen-science water testing kit which uses Vibrio Fischeri, a bioluminescent bacteria to determine the clenliness of a water sample. Thereby giving people control over their own health and safety.

This is an assembly of a piston comprised of five seperate parts. Each piece was sketched seperately before all five were combined into the final assembly. The orange crank shaft rotates on the green pin in the x/z plane. The red piston head is the body that houses the pin and shaft system. The blue collars are used to hold the pin in place inside the piston head. Each collar is nested into grooves on the exterior of piston head to ensure a tight fit.

This cannon was made out of one cylinder and one block of aluminum. A horizontal mill, vertical mill, lathe and band saw were used to cut the original pieces into the final product.
